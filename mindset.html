Stress

Damn right I like the life I live 'Cause I went from negative to positive.

The Notorious B.I.G., "Juicy" from "Notorious" soundtrack

In the past, I experienced a significant amount of distress at work primarily due to discrimintory politics from various "leaders" in an organization lacking integrity. I felt stuck at that job because I thought I first needed to get healthy before moving forward towards a better future. After a few years of consistent layoffs, I realized this stress negatively contributed to my health. This awareness lead to the realization that quitting my job was a prerequisite to becoming healthy. Now I work at my dream job and my workdays are filled with eustress, challenging problems, and collaboration with true technical leaders. Everyday, I do what I love and love what I do.

Stay Positive

When one's expectations are reduced to zero, one really appreciates everything one does have.

Stephen Hawking

After I was diagnosed with an incurable autoimmune disease, it was very easy for me to focus on negative aspects of my life. With proper expectations, I've realized my cup is still more than half-full. I've transformed the side-effects of an autoimmune disease into daily reminders to be thankful for what I have. The daily pain I experience is a constant reminder of my mortality. Those reminders guide me to efficiently prioritize, utilize, and appreciate the amazing gift I've been given: my life.

Future

The key to realizing a dream is to focus not on success but on significance, and then even the small steps and little victories along your path will take on greater meaning.

Oprah Winfrey

I recently realized that I have many perfectionist tendencies. I previously focused on results and what people thought about me. I was always trying my best to "fit in". This was a detrimental combination to my productivity and effectiveness. Now I've learned to focus on the process by taking one step at a time on a continuous daily basis. I am more comfortable to "stand out" rather than "fit in" since I choose to care less about what others think of me.
