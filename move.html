Weight Lifting

So no matter how hard it get,
stick your chest out, keep your head up, and handle it

Tupac Shakur, Me Against the World (1995)

I ended up consistently hurting myself weight lifting.  So I starting rock climbing to lift myself instead of weights.  Rock climbing builds core strength and is an enjoyable full-body problem solving activity.  In general static resistance training creates less pain and inflammation in my joints while still maintaining strength and muscle mass.

Walk / Swim

Even when I was close to defeat
I rose to my feet

Dr. Dre feat. Snoop Dogg, "Still D.R.E." from 2001

Running puts too much stress on my joints and was not sustainable for me in the long-term;  instead I've been walking, hiking, and swimming.  These activities are just as enjoyable and softer on my joints.  When I first started swimming, I had improper body position, and body rotation.  There's a lot of balance and coordination involved with swimming.  I learned how to properly swim from the experts at SwimSmooth.com.

Yoga

I never forgot Van Ness and Imperial
Look at my life, Ice Cube is a miracle

Ice Cube, "Gangster Rap Made Me Do It" (Raw Footage, 2008)

Yoga has helped me to increase mobility, focus my mind, live in the moment, and be thankful for every breathe which I usually take for granted throughout each day.  Think about right now, you're probably not as grateful for simply breathing compared to someone who is drowning or unable to breathe.  It's funny, many times we don't realize what we have to be thankful for until it's gone.  Being grateful for simply breathing has helped me to appreciate all the wonderful things in my life.  Yoga has helped me to properly utilize my mind for engineering tasks and problem solving through the self-discipline of focusing on my breathe for an extended period of time.  Focusing intently on something as boring breathe makes focusing on challenging subjects much easier.  I've used the Pomodoro technique to focus intently in 20-25 minute timespans to learn and solve complex problems.  Thus yoga has vicariously helped me to achieve quality of thought necessary for consistent intellectual breakthroughs to garner non-trivial accomplishments.
